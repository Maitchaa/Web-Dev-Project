Bird's Nest Fern (Asplenium nidus)

Bird’s Nest Ferns have crinkled, sword-shaped fronds that grow from a central rosette, resembling a bird’s nest. This unique look makes it a popular choice for adding texture and greenery to indoor spaces.

Watering

Water the Bird's Nest Fern when the soil feels slightly dry to the touch. These ferns prefer moist, but well-drained soil. They do not like to sit in standing water, so ensure proper drainage. Mist the leaves regularly to maintain humidity and prevent the soil from drying out too quickly.

Frequency

Water weekly, allowing the top inch of soil to dry out between waterings.

Technique

Water the soil directly and avoid pouring water into the central “nest” to prevent rot.

Soil

Use a rich, well-draining, slightly acidic potting mix. A mixture of peat and perlite is ideal for retaining moisture without waterlogging.

Light

Low to moderate, indirect light. Avoid direct sunlight, which can cause leaf burn.

Temperature

Thrives in temperatures between 65-80°F (18-27°C). Protect from cold drafts or sudden temperature changes.

Fertilization

Fertilize monthly during the growing season with a balanced, diluted liquid fertilizer.

Pruning

Minimal pruning is needed. Remove any damaged or dead fronds from the base to keep it tidy.

Humidity

High humidity is essential. Mist frequently or place near a humidifier to prevent fronds from drying out.

Air Circulation

Low to moderate. Avoid placing it in areas with strong airflow.

Pest Management

Bird’s Nest Ferns rarely have pest issues but may occasionally attract scale or aphids. Treat with insecticidal soap if needed.

Repotting

Repot every 1-2 years, or if the plant becomes rootbound. Choose a pot slightly larger than the current one to support growth.
