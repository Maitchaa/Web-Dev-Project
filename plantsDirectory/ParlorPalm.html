Parlor Palm (Chamaedorea elegans)

The Parlor Palm is an elegant, small palm that thrives indoors, making it an excellent choice for adding a touch of the tropics to any room. It’s low-maintenance and perfect for low-light areas.

Watering

The Parlor Palm prefers evenly moist soil. Water when the top inch of soil is dry, but avoid overwatering. These palms do not like sitting in water, so ensure proper drainage. They prefer slightly higher humidity, but they are more tolerant of low humidity than many other houseplants.

Frequency

Water weekly, allowing the top inch of soil to dry out between waterings.

Technique

Water thoroughly, then let the excess water drain out completely to prevent soggy soil.

Soil

A light, well-draining potting soil mix is ideal. Adding perlite can improve drainage and aeration.

Light

Prefers low to medium, indirect light. Avoid direct sunlight, which can cause leaf burn.

Temperature

Best in temperatures between 65-80°F (18-27°C). Protect from cold drafts and sudden temperature changes.

Fertilization

Feed monthly during the growing season with a balanced, diluted fertilizer. Fertilize less often in fall and winter.

Pruning

Trim dead or damaged fronds to keep the plant neat.

Humidity

Prefers moderate to high humidity. Mist occasionally or place near a humidifier for best results.

Air Circulation

Good airflow is beneficial to prevent mold or pest issues.

Pest Management

Watch for spider mites and mealybugs. Wipe fronds with a damp cloth regularly, and use insecticidal soap if pests appear.

Repotting

Repot every 2-3 years in spring if needed. Parlor Palms prefer being slightly rootbound, so don’t oversize the new pot.
