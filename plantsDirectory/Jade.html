Jade Plant (Crassula ovata)

The Jade Plant (Crassula ovata) is a popular, low-maintenance succulent known for its thick, fleshy leaves and tree-like appearance. It thrives in well-draining, sandy or cactus soil and prefers bright, indirect sunlight but can tolerate some direct light.

Watering

Jade plants are succulents (they hold water in their leaves), so they don't do well when sitting in constantly moist soil, so let the top 1 to 2 inches of soil dry out between waterings.

Frequency

Watering every 3 weeks is usually sufficient, but this can vary depending on the environment. Always allow the soil to dry out completely between waterings to avoid overwatering, which is a common mistake.

Technique

When you water, do so thoroughly until water drains out of the bottom of the pot. Ensure that the pot has drainage holes to allow excess water to escape.

Soil

A well-draining cactus or succulent mix is essential to prevent root rot. You can also create your own mix by combining regular potting soil with sand or perlite.

Light

Jade plants thrive in bright, indirect light but can tolerate some direct sunlight. Ideally, they should receive about 4-6 hours of light daily. If the leaves start to stretch or lose their vibrant color, it may be a sign that they need more light.

Fertilization

During the growing season (spring and summer), fertilize once a month with a diluted liquid fertilizer. This will encourage healthy growth and vibrant leaves.

Pruning

To maintain the shape of the plant and promote branching, trim back any overgrown stems. This can also help to rejuvenate the plant.

Humidity

Jade plants are quite adaptable and can tolerate low humidity levels, making them suitable for many indoor environments.

Air Circulation

Good airflow is crucial to prevent rot. Avoid placing the plant in a closed, humid environment.

Pest Management

While jade plants are generally pest-resistant, they can occasionally be affected by aphids or mealybugs. Regularly check the undersides of leaves and treat any infestations promptly.

Repotting

Repot every 2-3 years or when the plant becomes root-bound. Choose a pot that is slightly larger than the current one to allow for growth.
