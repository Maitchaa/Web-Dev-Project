ZZ Plant (Zamioculcas aureum)

The ZZ Plant (Zamioculcas zamiifolia) is a hardy, low-maintenance houseplant known for its glossy, dark green, waxy leaves and upright growth habit. It thrives in low to bright, indirect light and is highly tolerant of neglect, making it ideal for beginners or people with busy schedules. ZZ plants prefer infrequent watering, as they store water in their thick, tuberous roots; let the soil dry out completely between waterings. They are well-suited to average indoor temperatures and humidity levels. The ZZ Plant is also known for being a great air purifier, helping to remove toxins from the air.

Watering

ZZ plants are highly tolerant of dry conditions due to their thick, waxy leaves that help retain moisture. They store water in their rhizomes, making them very drought-tolerant.

Frequency

ZZ plants are drought-tolerant and should be watered when the soil is dry. Typically, this means watering every 2–3 weeks, depending on the environment’s temperature and humidity.

Technique

Water the ZZ plant thoroughly until water drains from the pot’s drainage holes. Ensure that the pot has good drainage to avoid water accumulating at the bottom, which can lead to root rot.

Soil

ZZ plants require well-draining soil to prevent root rot, as they are susceptible to overwatering. A cactus or succulent mix works well due to its light, airy texture. To further improve drainage, mix in perlite or coarse sand in a 1:1 ratio with the potting mix.

Light

PZZ plants thrive in bright, indirect light but can adapt to low light conditions. They are often used in spaces with minimal natural light due to their ability to survive in low-light environments.

Temperature

ZZ plants prefer temperatures between 60–75°F (15–24°C), which is typical of most indoor environments. They are not frost-tolerant and should be kept in areas that stay above 45°F (7°C).

Fertilization

During the growing season (spring and summer), fertilize once a month with a balanced, diluted liquid fertilizer, ideally with an NPK ratio of 10-10-10. A weak solution helps encourage healthy growth without overloading the plant with nutrients.

Pruning

ZZ plants are slow-growing, so pruning is typically minimal. Remove yellowing, dead, or damaged leaves by cutting them off at the base with sharp scissors or pruning shears.

Humidity

ZZ plants are adaptable to a variety of humidity levels. They can thrive in low humidity environments, making them an excellent choice for homes and offices with dry air.

Air Circulation

ZZ plants do not require high airflow but benefit from moderate circulation to keep the leaves dust-free and healthy.

Pest Management

ZZ plants are naturally pest-resistant, but they can occasionally be affected by pests like aphids, mealybugs, or scale. Aphids are often found on new growth, while mealybugs can accumulate in the leaf joints.

Repotting

ZZ plants do not need frequent repotting and should only be repotted when they become root-bound. This usually happens every 2-3 years. They are slow growers, so they do not outgrow their pots quickly.
